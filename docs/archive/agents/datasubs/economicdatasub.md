# Economic Data Subagent

## Overview
The Economic Data Subagent specializes in macroeconomic data collection, analysis, and interpretation. It aggregates economic indicators, policy data, and financial market signals to provide comprehensive macroeconomic intelligence for trading and investment decisions.

## Core Capabilities

### Economic Indicator Collection
- **GDP and Growth Data**: Real GDP, GDP growth rates, and economic output metrics
- **Inflation Measures**: CPI, PPI, core inflation, and inflation expectations
- **Employment Data**: Unemployment rates, job creation, wage growth, and labor market indicators
- **Trade and Balance Data**: Trade balances, current account, capital flows

### Monetary Policy Analysis
- **Interest Rates**: Federal funds rate, treasury yields, LIBOR, and global rates
- **Central Bank Communications**: FOMC minutes, speeches, and policy statements
- **Quantitative Easing**: Asset purchase programs and balance sheet changes
- **Forward Guidance**: Policy rate projections and economic outlook assessments

### Financial Market Indicators
- **Yield Curve Analysis**: Treasury yield curve shapes and inversion signals
- **Credit Spreads**: Corporate bond spreads and credit market conditions
- **Currency Markets**: Exchange rates, currency volatility, and carry trade opportunities
- **Commodity Prices**: Energy, metals, agricultural commodities, and inflation signals

## Data Sources

### Official Economic Data
- **Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS)**: Employment, inflation, and productivity data
- **Bureau of Economic Analysis (BEA)**: GDP, personal income, and trade data
- **Federal Reserve**: Monetary policy data, FOMC communications, and economic projections
- **Treasury Department**: Budget data, debt levels, and international capital flows

### Financial Data Providers
- **FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data)**: Comprehensive economic database
- **Bloomberg**: Real-time economic indicators and market data
- **Reuters**: Economic news and data feeds
- **Haver Analytics**: Global economic and financial data

### Alternative Economic Indicators
- **High-Frequency Data**: Daily economic activity indicators
- **Private Sector Surveys**: Purchasing managers indices and business sentiment
- **Geopolitical Data**: Trade tensions, policy changes, and global risk factors
- **Social Media Sentiment**: Economic sentiment from social platforms

## LLM Integration

### Economic Analysis and Interpretation
- **Policy Impact Assessment**: Evaluating central bank actions and their market implications
- **Economic Narrative Generation**: Creating coherent stories from disparate economic data
- **Forward Guidance Analysis**: Interpreting central bank communications and projections
- **Market Reaction Prediction**: Anticipating market responses to economic data releases

### Research Workflow
1. **Data Aggregation**: Collect economic indicators from multiple official sources
2. **Policy Analysis**: Evaluate central bank communications and policy implications
3. **LLM Deep Dive**: Comprehensive analysis of economic conditions and market impacts
4. **Signal Generation**: Identify actionable trading signals from economic data
5. **Collaborative Sharing**: Distribute economic insights to other data subagents

## Collaborative Memory System

### Memory Architecture
- **Economic Cycle Analysis**: Maintains knowledge of economic cycles and turning points
- **Policy Response Patterns**: Stores historical central bank reactions to economic conditions
- **Market Correlations**: Tracks relationships between economic data and asset prices
- **Indicator Reliability**: Accumulates performance data for different economic indicators

### Memory Management
- **Session-Based Storage**: Memory exists only during active research sessions
- **Collaborative Sharing**: Enables cross-subagent validation and enhancement
- **Base Agent Transfer**: Delivers economic analysis with complete context
- **Cleanup Protocol**: Automatic deletion of temporary economic data after transfer

## Economic Analysis Framework

### Leading Indicators
